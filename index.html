ARCH/GARCH and LSTM Comparison On Forecasting The Sea Level On Marina Ancol 2022-2023

Status: Completed on December 2023

This team-based project, undertaken as the final assignment for the Time Series Forecasting Methods course, focuses on comparing the effectiveness of ARCH/GARCH and LSTM models in forecasting sea levels at Marina Ancol for the period 2022-2023. Our goal is to determine which model offers the most accurate and reliable predictions, thereby providing valuable insights into the application of time series analysis for environmental forecasting.

The findings of this project have significant implications for environmental forecasting and management. Accurate sea level predictions are crucial for coastal planning, flood prevention, and climate change mitigation efforts. By identifying the most effective forecasting method, this project contributes to enhancing the accuracy and reliability of environmental predictions.

This project not only fulfills the requirements of the Time Series Forecasting Methods course but also provides a comprehensive analysis of the comparative effectiveness of ARCH/GARCH and LSTM models in sea level forecasting. Our findings will be valuable for researchers, environmentalists, and policymakers involved in coastal management and climate change adaptation strategies.

In summary, this project demonstrates the power of combining traditional statistical methods with modern deep learning techniques to tackle complex environmental forecasting challenges, thereby contributing to the advancement of time series analysis and its applications in real-world scenarios.
